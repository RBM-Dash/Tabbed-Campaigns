Definitions and Data Sources

Campaigns distributing insecticide-treated mosquito nets (ITN), indoor residual spraying (IRS), as well as preventative treatments for children (SMC) and pregnant women have contributed to saving more than 7 million lives and preventing over 1 billion new malaria infections since 2000. It is essential that these campaigns and routine community-level interventions are not disrupted to avoid a surge in malaria cases, especially in Africa, a continent which accounts for over 90% of the global malaria burden.

The Campaign Dashboard provides a global overview on the number of planned campaigns bby intervention and status by country. Additional details on the commodity status of essential medications and funding gaps can be found on the Supply Chain and Financial GAPS Dashboards

Definitions and Data sources
Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention (SMC) was recommended by WHO in 2012 to prevent malaria in children less than 5 years living in areas of highly seasonal malaria transmission across the Sahel sub-region of Africa. It is a safe and cost-effective strategy that can be easily administered by health workers and community health workers at the community level. As such, it has been well accepted and widely deployed by national malaria control programs (NMCPs), government decision-makers, and local communities. In 2014, fewer than 5% (~3 million) of all eligible children benefited from SMC

At Risk:Some risk that the campaign wil run into delays/not take place in calendar year planned;a situation not yet critical but important to watch and ensure that the bottlenecks do not grow and move the country off-track

OnTrack:The campaign is on track to take place in the same Calendar year a initially planned/there are no significant bottlenecks identified as of the last report date that would move the campaign off track

Definitions and Data sources
Indoor residual spraying or IRS is an effective intervention to prevent Malaria transmission consisting on spraying the inside of dwellings with a dilute solution of insecticide to kill or repel mosquitoes.

Definitions and Data sources
Insecticide-treated bed nets (ITNs) reduce malaria illness, severe disease, and death due to malaria in endemic regions. Long-lasting insecticide-treated nets (LLINs) maintain effective levels of insecticide for at least 3 years, even after repeated washing. WHO now recommends that LLINs be distributed to and used by all people (“universal coverage”) in malarious areas every 3 years.
